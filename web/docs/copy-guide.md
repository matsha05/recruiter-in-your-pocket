# RIYP — Tone & Copy Manual

This is the definitive voice guide for Recruiter in Your Pocket. Every string on the site (marketing, in-app, legal, transactional) should sound like it came from the same person.

---

## The Voice

You are a **sharp recruiter friend**. Not a corporate product. Not a chatbot. Not a blog.

You've read thousands of resumes. You know what gets someone hired and what gets them skipped. You're warm, direct, and a little opinionated. You've earned it.

### The persona in one sentence

> A recruiter who actually cares, pulling you aside to tell you what's really going on with your resume.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Talk to one person

Write "you" and "your," not "users" or "candidates." Every sentence should feel like it's spoken across a table.

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| Users can upload their resumes | Drop your resume to find out |
| Candidates receive actionable insights | You'll see exactly what to fix |

### 2. Lead with what they get, not how it works

Nobody cares about your architecture. They care about the result. Say what happens for them first. Explain the mechanism only if it builds trust.

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| Our heuristics found high verb impact | Your verbs are sharp and your outcomes are specific |
| We extract evidence before advice | We read the evidence first, then tell you what to do |

### 3. Be specific, never vague

Vague copy sounds like every other product. Specific copy sounds like you know what you're doing.

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| Get actionable insights | See the exact line that's hurting you, and how to fix it |
| Improve your resume | Write bullets that sound like you owned the work |

### 4. Earn the confidence

State opinions. Make claims. But back them up. "Based on how recruiters really read resumes" is confident AND credible.

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| We might be able to help improve your resume | We'll show you what a recruiter thinks when they read it |
| Results may vary | Your first review is free. See if it's useful. |

### 5. Kill the jargon

If a phrase would never come out of your mouth in conversation, cut it.

**Banned phrases:**
- "evidence-linked actions"
- "confidence bands"
- "signal extraction"
- "evidence-backed"
- "heuristics"
- "normalizing structure"
- "priority ordering"
- "positioning notes"
- "recruiter signal"
- "copy-ready"
- "action items" (in marketing; fine in the in-app report itself)
- "key insights"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "optimize" / "optimization"
- "unlock your potential"
- "actionable insights"
- "data-driven"
- "best-in-class"
- "utilize"

**Banned punctuation:**
- Em dashes (—). Use periods, commas, or restructure instead. Em dashes are a crutch for avoiding commitment to a sentence structure. Pick one and commit.

**Use instead:**
- "what to fix" / "how to fix it"
- "what a recruiter thinks"
- "proof of impact"
- "what stands out, what falls flat"
- "phrasing a recruiter would notice"
- "the exact line"
- "why this matters"
- "how confident we are"

## Tone by Surface

### Marketing (Landing page, Pricing, Research hub)

**Warm, confident, slightly opinionated.** This is where you make promises. Be bold but never slimy.

> "In 7.4 seconds, they've already made up their mind about you."

- Lead with a hook that creates a small amount of productive anxiety
- Immediately follow with reassurance and specificity
- Use "we" sparingly and always in service of "you"

### In-App (Report, Scanning, Gated states)

**Calm, clear, supportive.** The user just uploaded something personal. Don't sell here. Help.

> "This is what a recruiter sees."  
> "The stories you forgot to tell."  
> "How I'd rewrite these to land harder."

- Use the recruiter persona voice ("How I'd rewrite these")
- Section titles should feel like observations, not product labels
- Loading states should feel like something useful is happening, not a tech demo

### Legal & Trust (FAQ, Privacy, Terms, Security, Methodology)

**Plain, direct, honest.** These pages build trust by being unusually human for their category.

> "We don't sell your data. We don't train on it. We don't share it."  
> "ATS tools check if a parser can read your file. We focus on what a human recruiter really thinks."

- Write like you're answering a real question someone asked
- Default to contractions: "don't" over "do not"
- No defensive language. Confidence, not hedging.

### Transactional (Purchase, Restore, Settings, Error states)

**Efficient, reassuring, no-fuss.** The user is dealing with money or account stuff. Be clear and fast.

> "We saved your place in The Red Pen."  
> "Need help now? Email support@recruiterinyourpocket.com and include your checkout email."

- State what happened, then state what to do next
- Never make the user feel stupid for hitting an error
- Include exactly one helpful next step

## The "Actually" Rule

"Actually" is a powerful word. It implies contrast: "the truth vs. what you assumed." Use it **at most 2–3 times** across the entire site. When overused, it becomes a tic and loses its punch.

✅ **Good use:** "Your resume should show what you're actually great at"  
❌ **Bad use:** "What recruiters actually read" + "What they actually think" + "How they actually scan" (same page)

## Headline Patterns That Work

1. **The reveal:** "In 7.4 seconds, they've already made up their mind about you."
2. **The question:** "Want to know exactly how we handle your data?"
3. **The observation:** "The stories you forgot to tell."
4. **The claim:** "Built with people who hire for a living."
5. **The contrast:** "Honest, useful feedback. Not robotic."

Avoid:
- Headlines that start with "Welcome to..."
- Headlines that are just feature names ("Evidence Ledger")
- Headlines followed by subheadlines that say the same thing differently

## CTA Button Copy

Keep buttons to **2–4 words**. Front-load the verb.

| Context | Button Copy |
|---|---|
| Primary CTA | Run Your Free Review |
| Run another | Run Another |
| Upgrade | Get More Reviews |
| Gated section | Unlock the Evidence Ledger |
| After completion | Jump to Rewrites |
| Export | Download PDF |

Avoid: "Submit," "Learn More," "Get Started," "Click Here."

---

## Proofreading Checklist

Before shipping any copy, check:

- [ ] Would I say this out loud to a friend? If no, rewrite.
- [ ] Am I leading with the user's benefit, not the feature?
- [ ] Is there any banned jargon? Search the banned list.
- [ ] Does "actually" appear more than 3 times on the page?
- [ ] Is every headline specific enough that it could only belong to this product?
- [ ] Are my CTAs action-first and under 4 words?
- [ ] Does the gated-state copy sell the unlock without feeling desperate?
